Assignment #6

Playing an Instrument

  • Course Home

Create an audio/visual musical instrument. The instrument should make sound and display visuals based on user interaction (mouse movements, key presses, etc). Make sure that interactions and their outcomes are predictable enough that a user can practice and become proficient at “playing” your instrument (i.e it cannot be too random). Your instrument can trigger snippets or sound files, or generate sound algorithmically. You may use Processing or Max.

Once you have a working prototype, invite at least two people to interact with it (preferably, not fellow DMI students). Document these interactions visually, and make notes on how your users experienced your piece.

Adapted from Alison Kotin's & Nicole Tariverdian's Move/change assignment.

Assignment 6.0 (~30min.)

  1. Download the SonicPainterDesktop example. It is located in examples 7|processing|maxim|1_Intro_To_Audio|SonicPainterDesktop. Or just (download as zip).
  2. Run SonicPainterDesktop and see how it behaves. It changes when you press and hold your mouse button.
  3. Look at the source code. Try to figure out how various aspects are working. Change parameters, variables and anything that is a number. See how this affects the output.

Assignment 6.1 (~15-50min.)

  1. Make a copy of SonicPainterDesktop by clicking File|Save as…, name it a6.1. Save to your eom-f16-student folder.
  2. Change the visuals, instead of lines, how about blocks or circles. Remember assignment #3, A Drawing Tool?
  3. Commit your a6.1 Sketch to Github.
  4. Videotape a user interacting with your Sketch. Upload to Youtube, create a link on your Github page.

Assignment 6.2 (~15-30min.)

  1. Starting from a6.1 make a new copy of your Sketch called a6.2
  2. Change how the Sketch sounds by replacing the .wav files in the data directory with new sound files.
  3. Commit your a6.2 Sketch to Github.
  4. Videotape a user interacting with your Sketch. Upload to Youtube, create a link on your Github page.

Assignment 6.3 (~15-30min.)

  1. Starting from a6.2 make a new copy of your Sketch called a6.3
  2. Change how the Sketch sounds by editing the values being set in setFilter, ramp and speed in the Sketch source code.
  3. Commit your a6.3 Sketch to Github.
  4. Videotape a user interacting with your Sketch. Upload to Youtube, create a link on your Github page.
